Victoria's waste recovery journey

Tracing trends & change

The patterns in Victoria's waste recovery data reveal a number of stories about our transition to a circular economy:

  • While waste per capita has increased moderately, a rising recovery rate has meant recovered volumes have improved.
  • The rollout of kerbside food and organics collection services (from 2020 onwards) has driven organics recovery and contributed to a rise in recovered municipal waste.
  • Victoria's COVID lockdowns caused a notable bump in waste generated, as Victorian's had more good delivered.
  • Changes in material recovery rates are driven by the maturity of reprocessing industries and the relative prices of virgin vs recovered materials. There remains a strong economic case for recycling metals and building products.

All volume changes are shown on a per capita basis to account for Victoria's population growth over the period. Line weights are scaled to show the relative volume of each sector and material over the period.
