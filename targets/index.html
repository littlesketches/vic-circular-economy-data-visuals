Closing the gap to

Victoria's 2030
Waste Targets

Recycling Victoria: a new economy policy was released in 2020. This was a new plan to establish a recycling system Victorians could rely upon and transform how our economy uses materials. Four ambitious targets were set to help Victoria measure our progress towards a circular economy:

  1. Divert 80 per cent of waste from landfill by 2030, and an interim target of 72 per cent by 2025.
  2. Cut total waste generation by 15 per cent per capita by 2030.
  3. Halve the volume of organic material going to landfill between 2020 and 2030, with an interim target of 20 per cent reduction by 2025.
  4. Ensure every Victorian household has access to food and garden organic waste recycling services or local composting by 2030.

All targets are scaled from their 2020 baseline, with the exception of recovery rate whose interim target is set at the halfway point of the 2020—2030 target period.
