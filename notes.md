## Headline metrics 
Taken from the Circular Economy Market Report

- Circularity rate % 
    - measures the percentage of all consumed materials that are recycled
    -  an indicator of material reuse. 
    - Secondary materials consumption (SMC) / Domestic materials consumption (DMC) in Victoria

- Material footprint [tonnes per peron]
    - measures virgin(raw) material demand per capita within an economy
    - indicator of overall environmental sustainability

- Material productivity [ $ per kg of material consumed]
    - quantifies the economic output or value added per unit of materials consumed

### Supporting metrics
- Waste generation vs waste recovery [volume and volume per capita]
- $ value of waste recovered and sent to landfill
- Sector has a 'value'

### The four targets
1. Divert 80% of waste from landfill by 2030, with an interim target of 72% by 2025.
    - Total Waste collectd
2. Cut total waste generation by 15% per capita by 2030.
    - 
3. Halve the volume of organic materials going to landfill between 2020–2030, with an interim target of 20% reduction by 2025.
4. Ensure every household has access to food and garden organic waste recycling services or local composting by 2030.

### Barriers to circularity
- Contamination – high processing costs associated with sorting, decontaminating and cleaning material streams.
- Limited end markets – weak local demand for reusing, reprocessing or recycling some material streams.
- Product design – products are not designed for circularity or end-of-life. Complex product designs, the use of mixed materials, and the difficulty of disassembling products hinder effective recycling.
- Competition with international jurisdictions: Victorian recyclers and re-manufacturers compete with international jurisdictions that have lower costs and stronger manufacturing bases.
- Competition with virgin materials – cheaper virgin materials, particularly those derived from fossil fuels, often undermine the competitiveness of recycled alternatives.
